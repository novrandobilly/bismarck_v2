# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences:

**Customers** (primary): Friends, community members, and the general public ordering bagels via their smartphone browser. They're arriving from a social post or a shared link, likely on mobile, in a casual context — curious about what's available and how to place a pre-order.

**Owner/Admin** (secondary): The bagel maker themselves, managing sessions, orders, menu items, and payments via a desktop or laptop. The admin experience should be functional and clear, not a design showcase.

## Product Purpose

A pre-order management system for a small, homemade sourdough bagel shop. Customers browse the menu and place pre-orders tied to specific pickup sessions. The owner creates and manages those sessions, tracks orders, and coordinates fulfillment.

Success looks like: a customer lands from Instagram, feels immediately that this is a real, warm, handmade thing they want to support, finds an open session, and completes their order without friction.

## Brand Personality

Fresh, cozy, artisanal.

A small home industry that's close and friendly with its customers and community. Not polished like a corporate chain — more like a neighborhood bagel maker who knows your name. Homemade, genuine, inviting.

Inspired by: The Wildman Bagel (Hiroshima) — a small, community-rooted bagel shop active on Instagram.

## Anti-references

- Big corporate fast-food aesthetics (McDonald's, GrabFood, GoFood) — impersonal, production-line feel
- Generic SaaS/startup design — gradients, glassmorphism, hero metrics, growth-tool vibes
- Overly minimal Japanese clean-grid — cold, distant, too designed to feel handmade

## Design Principles

1. **Neighborhood warmth** — every screen should feel like a note from a friend, not a transaction from a corporation. Approachable language, human imperfection over clinical precision.
2. **Mobile-first intimacy** — customers are on their phones. Every touchpoint (menu browsing, ordering, proof upload) should feel natural at thumb reach, not adapted from desktop.
3. **Handmade over high-production** — slight warmth, organic rhythm, and texture over perfect grids. A menu card should feel like a chalkboard menu, not a product listing.
4. **Honest and direct** — no dark patterns, no aggressive upselling, no confusing flows. People trust this baker; the app should reinforce that trust.
5. **Community over conversion** — the tone is welcoming and generous, not sales-pressured. The CTA is an invitation, not a push.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG level requirement. Prioritize readability and usability: sufficient contrast on all text, touch targets sized for thumbs on mobile, legible type at small sizes.
